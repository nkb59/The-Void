The Bathtub on the Balcony: The Murder of Lindsay Hawker

In March 2007, a 22-year-old English teacher from Coventry went for a coffee with a man she'd just met in a Chiba train station. She never came home. What police found on his apartment balcony the next day set off one of the largest manhunts in Japan's history, and it would take more than two years, a trail of self-inflicted plastic surgery, and one clinic's tip to close the case.

Show notes

Lindsay Ann Hawker was born in December 1984 and grew up in Coventry, England. In October 2006 she moved to Japan to teach English at a branch of Nova, then the country's largest private language school, and shared a flat with two other foreign teachers.

On the evening of March 25, 2007, Lindsay met 28-year-old Tatsuya Ichihashi. Accounts of how they met vary, but the two ended up sharing a taxi to his apartment in Ichikawa, Chiba Prefecture. She asked the driver to wait outside. He didn't see her again.

The next day, police entering Ichihashi's apartment found it empty of him but full of Lindsay's belongings. He had bolted past several officers stationed outside and vanished into the city. Her body was discovered on the apartment's balcony the following day, submerged in a bathtub filled with a mixture of sand and soil, bound and gagged. She had been beaten and, investigators would later confirm, raped.

What followed was a manhunt that stretched past two and a half years. An outdated photo on the original wanted poster hampered the search early on, and Ichihashi moved constantly, working odd jobs and, according to his own later account, cutting and stitching his own face in an attempt to change his appearance. Lindsay's parents, Bill and Julia, traveled to Japan repeatedly to keep pressure on the investigation.

Ichihashi was finally arrested in November 2009 at a ferry terminal in Osaka, after a cosmetic surgery clinic flagged a suspicious patient to police. In 2011 he confessed to smothering Lindsay during the assault to stop her from screaming. He was convicted and sentenced to life in prison that July.
